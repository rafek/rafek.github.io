developing

Sometime before I started my professional career as a software developer I read a book that had a great impact on how I would think and act as a professional. The Pragmatic Programmer: From Journeyman to Master is packed with good practices for software craftsmen, especially newcomers. One of the sentences that struck me the most was: "You shouldn't be wedded to any particular technology, but have a broad enough background and experience base to allow you to choose good solutions in particular situations.". That one really stuck with me.

Being in the industry since 2007 and working for several different organizations allowed me to gain a fair amount of experience in a variety of technologies and work environments. I worked for companies of different sizes and industries. Within teams that were fully on-site or distributed globally. I started with .NET based desktop applications, then moved to Microsoft web stack where I endured both sides of ASP.NET: WebForms and MVC. After delivering more than a handful of web apps, I jumped into a mobile world and got involved in native iOS development followed by projects in cross-platform solutions like Cordova or Xamarin. I also spent a decent time in a glorious kingdom of JavaScript doing backend and frontend. As of 2017, I do mostly AR and VR with Windows Mixed Reality platform. Occasional Python backend development is also a good treat.

“The process of learning will expand your thinking, opening you to new possibilities and new ways of doing things.” ~ The Pragmatic Programmer: From Journeyman to Master
